Asbestos refers to six naturally occurring minerals that are mined from the earth. They include amosite, chrysotile, tremolite, actinolite, anthophyllite, and crocidolite.
Chrysotile or white asbestos is the most common variety of asbestos found in North America. It accounts for approximately 90-95% of asbestos that was used in commercial applications in the United States.
Asbestos-related diseases have a long latency period. That means many of the people dying from mesothelioma today were exposed to asbestos in the 1960s and 1970s.
Asbestos poses a health risk when its fibers are released into the air and inhaled or ingested. These fibers are too small to be seen by the naked eye—some individual fibers may be up to 700 times smaller than a human hair.
