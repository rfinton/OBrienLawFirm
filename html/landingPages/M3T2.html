While these six types of asbestos have physical and chemical differences, the U.S Department of Health and Human Services, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and the International Agency for Research on Cancer classify all types of asbestos as carcinogens and hazardous to human health.

History of Asbestos

The word asbestos derives from ancient Greek, meaning "inextinguishable". Asbestos use dates back at least 4,500 years - evidence shows that inhabitants of the Lake Juojärvi region in East Finland strengthened earthenware pots and cooking utensils with the asbestos mineral.

- Source: Asbestos Disease Awareness Organization
  • 2000 BC
    Egyptians use asbestos to make burial shrouds.
  • 1828
    First U.S. patent for asbestos is issued.
  • 1860's
    Use of asbestos in industry and construction expands dramatically, exposing large numbers of workers to asbestos dust.
  • 1906
    Dr. Montague Murray reported an uncommonly high mortality rate amongst asbestos workers.
  • 1918
    Insurance companies began refusing to sell insurance to companies employing asbestos workers.
  • 1922
    U.S. Navy lists asbestos work as hazardous and recommends the use of respirators.
  • 1924
    Dr. W. E. Cooke names the lung disease associated with asbestos as "asbestosis".
  • 1929
    Workers begin filing workers comp claims against Johns-Manville for lung damage caused by asbestos.
  • 1940
    Industry-backed research carried out at the Saranac Laboratory in New York showed that 80% of mice that inhaled asbestos fibers developed pulmonary cancer. This research was not published.
  • 1943
    Dr. Leroy Gardner reports asbestos a “Likely Carcinogen”.
  • 1955
    Dr. Richard Doll publishes study linking asbestos to lung cancer.
  • 1960
    Dr. J.C. Wagner published studies confirming a unique cancer was associated with asbestos exposure, identified as "mesothelioma."
  • 1964
    Dr. Irving Selikoff reports that all trades and occupation that work with asbestos, as well as household exposures are at risk of developing asbestos disease.
  • 1969
    Borel v. Fibreboard Paper Products Corp. was the first civil lawsuit to be filed against asbestos product manufacturers.
  • 1970
    Occupational Safety and Health Act passed resulting in the first government-sanctioned workplace standard for asbestos in 1972.
  • 1971
    OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration) and NIOSH (National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health) created the first standard for regulating asbestos exposure. EPA lists asbestos as a hazardous air pollutant.
  • 1973
    On appeal, the Borel v. Fibreboard Paper Products Corp. was the first legal case confirming that manufacturers have a duty to warn product users about the dangers of asbestos dangers.
  • 1976
    NIOSH calls for a ban on asbestos in U.S. workplaces.
  • 1976
    Congress passed The Toxic Substances Control Act of 1976, which gave EPA authority to test and regulate toxic chemicals including asbestos.
